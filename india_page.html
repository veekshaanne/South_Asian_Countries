The Land of Diversity and Heritage

India is a vast South Asian republic stretching from the Himalayas to the Indian Ocean, home to a highly diverse population, many languages, and multiple religions. Its capital is New Delhi, and the country is known for deep historical traditions combined with rapid modern development.

Explore India

Culture & Traditions

Indian culture emphasizes family, community, and religious life, with Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism all shaping social customs and festivals.

Festivals such as Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, and regional harvest celebrations are marked by rituals, decorations, and communal meals across the country.

Cuisine

Indian cuisine is famous for its use of spices and regional diversity, broadly grouped into northern, southern, eastern, western, and northeastern styles of cooking.

Popular dishes include biryani, butter chicken, masala dosa, samosas, tandoori items, and sweets such as gulab jamun and jalebi, with rice more common in the south and wheat in much of the north.
