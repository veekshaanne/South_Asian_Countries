Culture & Traditions

Maldivian culture reflects South Asian, Arabian, and East African influences shaped by centuries of seafaring and trade along Indian Ocean routes.

Islam is the state religion and central to public life and law, and many customs and holidays follow the Islamic lunar calendar, with mosques prominent in island communities.

Cuisine

Maldivian cuisine, often called Dhivehi cuisine, centers on fish- especially tuna- coconut, and starchy foods like taro, breadfruit, and rice, often served as curries and stews.

Typical dishes include mas huni (tuna with grated coconut and chili), fish curries such as mas riha, clear fish soup called garudhiya, and snacks like bajiya and gulha eaten with tea.

Folklore & Beliefs

Maldivian folklore includes legends of sea demons and spirits said to haunt islands and reefs, part of a rich oral tradition that predates and coexists with Islamic beliefs.
