🇳🇵 Nepal

Himalayan Land of Peaks and Temples

Nepal is a landlocked Himalayan nation between India and China known for dramatic mountain landscapes, including Mount Everest. It is home to diverse cultures, religions, and languages, making it one of South Asia’s most culturally rich countries.

Explore Nepal

Culture & Traditions
Nepal blends Hindu and Buddhist traditions seen in temples, monasteries, and festivals. Major celebrations include Dashain and Tihar, celebrated across the country. Traditional dances, music, and art remain important cultural expressions.
Cuisine
A typical Nepali meal consists of dal bhat (lentils and rice) with vegetables or meat. Momos (dumplings) are one of the most popular foods across the country. Regional dishes also include sel roti, gundruk, and thukpa.
Tourist Spots
Nepal attracts visitors for trekking in the Everest and Annapurna regions. Kathmandu Valley features historic cities like Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur. Religious sites such as Pashupatinath Temple and Boudhanath Stupa draw pilgrims and tourists.
Leaders (Past–Present)
Nepal was historically ruled by monarchs until becoming a republic in 2008. It now operates as a federal democratic republic with an elected parliament and president.
Folklore & Epics
Nepalese folklore includes mountain spirits, local gods, and heroic legends. Many stories are connected to sacred Himalayan landscapes and ancient temples.
Quick Facts
Nepal contains eight of the world’s ten highest mountains including Mount Everest. The capital city is Kathmandu. Tourism and agriculture are major sectors of the economy.
Geography & Climate
Nepal ranges from lowland plains called the Terai to the Himalayan mountains. The climate varies from tropical in the south to alpine conditions in the north.
Languages & Diversity
Nepali is the official language. More than 120 languages and numerous ethnic groups contribute to the country’s diversity.
Economy & Modern Nepal
Nepal’s economy depends on tourism, agriculture, and remittances. Infrastructure and hydropower development are important goals for modern growth.
Wildlife & Nature
Nepal has national parks like Chitwan and Sagarmatha. Wildlife includes Bengal tigers, rhinos, snow leopards, and Himalayan birds.
Famous Monument
Boudhanath Stupa is one of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world and a major pilgrimage site in Kathmandu.
