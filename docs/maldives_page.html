🇲🇻 Maldives

Island Nation of Atolls and Lagoons

The Maldives is a tropical island nation in the Indian Ocean made up of coral atolls and lagoons. It is known worldwide for its beaches, luxury resorts, coral reefs, and diverse marine life.

Explore Maldives

Culture & Traditions
Maldivian culture is influenced by South Asia, Arabia, and East Africa due to centuries of maritime trade. Islam became the state religion in the 12th century and remains central to social life and traditions. Traditional music called **Bodu Beru** features drums and rhythmic dancing performed at celebrations.
Cuisine
Maldivian cuisine uses fish, coconut, and rice as its primary ingredients. Mas Huni, a mixture of tuna, coconut, onion, and chili, is a popular breakfast dish. Garudhiya, a fish broth served with rice and lime, is a traditional meal.
Tourist Spots
Famous destinations include North Malé Atoll, Ari Atoll, and Baa Atoll. Tourists visit the Maldives for scuba diving, snorkeling, and luxury island resorts. Clear lagoons and coral reefs attract marine life enthusiasts from around the world.
Leaders (Past–Present)
The Maldives is a presidential republic. The president serves as both head of state and government. The country has undergone democratic reforms in recent decades.
Folklore & Epics
Traditional Maldivian folklore includes legends of sea spirits and mythical creatures. The famous story of **Rannamaari**, a sea demon, is part of island folklore. These stories were passed down orally through generations.
Quick Facts
The Maldives has about 1,200 islands grouped into 26 atolls. The capital city is Malé. It is the lowest country in the world by elevation.
Geography & Climate
The Maldives lies southwest of India in the Indian Ocean. Its islands are coral-based and surrounded by lagoons and reefs. The climate is tropical with warm temperatures year-round.
Languages & Diversity
The official language of the Maldives is **Dhivehi**. English is widely spoken, especially in tourism and business sectors.
Economy & Modern Maldives
Tourism is the largest industry in the Maldives. Fishing is also a major economic activity, especially tuna fishing. The country is investing in sustainable tourism and marine conservation.
Wildlife & Nature
The Maldives is home to coral reefs, manta rays, sea turtles, and whale sharks. Marine biodiversity is one of the country's greatest natural assets.
Famous Monument
One of the most famous landmarks is the **Grand Friday Mosque** in Malé. It is known for its golden dome and beautiful Islamic architecture.
